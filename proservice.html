ProService North Star:
The Admin Dashboard Redesign

ProService's Admin Dashboard redesign was a pivotal project aimed at modernizing the HR experience for administrators while aligning with key business objectives. I led the UX strategy, conducted research with internal teams and end users, and designed a streamlined interface focused on clarity, navigation, and essential functionality.

Challenge

An outdated dashboard that couldn't keep up

The existing Admin Dashboard at ProService was outdated, unintuitive, and failed to meet the evolving needs of HR administrators. Users expressed frustration with poor navigation, limited functionality, and a lack of actionable insights — hindering their ability to manage critical HR tasks efficiently.

These shortcomings not only impacted user satisfaction but also limited ProService's ability to differentiate in the market and achieve strategic goals for growth and adoption.

UX Research

Grounding every decision in real user data

I began with a heuristic evaluation of the existing dashboard to identify usability issues, followed by stakeholder interviews with Subject Matter Experts, Sales, and Marketing to ensure alignment with business goals. User interviews with 10 HR admins across various roles provided the critical insights that shaped the design.

Data analysis of support tickets, NPS summaries, and Salesforce data complemented qualitative research, surfacing recurring pain points at scale.

Key Findings

Five patterns that shaped the design

01
Navigation inefficiency
Common tasks required excessive clicks. Admins spent more time finding tools than using them.
02
Buried data
Critical metrics and reports were hidden under multiple layers, slowing data-driven decisions.
03
No customization
The fixed interface couldn't adapt to different admin roles — one size fit none.
04
Poor error handling
Frequent errors with no clear feedback left admins without resolution paths.
05
Varied usage patterns
Some admins used the dashboard daily; others monthly for specific tasks. Both groups needed to be served.
Solution

A scalable foundation, not a one-time fix

I led end-to-end design for the redesign, managing a designer to execute the visual system. The focus was on a modern, self-service experience that could scale without increasing headcount while maintaining a professional first impression.

Key design decisions: streamlined navigation to reduce clicks, Favorites functionality for personalization, Employee Management tools tailored to admin feedback, and data visualizations — charts and graphs — to surface actionable insights at a glance.

The Figma architecture used scalable component patterns, ensuring future teams could extend rather than rebuild.

Outcome

Measurable improvement, positioned for growth

The redesign delivered a modern, streamlined interface that prioritized usability and data-driven decision-making. Early feedback from internal stakeholders and admins indicated a significant improvement in user experience.

Simplified workflows reduced task completion time for key HR tasks. Improved data accessibility enabled faster, data-driven decisions. Tailored customization options improved adoption across both power users and occasional users. Consistent ProService brand integration reinforced credibility across the platform.
