ProService North Star:
The Admin Dashboard Redesign

ProService's Admin Dashboard redesign was a pivotal project aimed at modernizing the HR experience for administrators while aligning with strategic business objectives. I led UX strategy, conducted research with internal teams and end users, and designed a streamlined interface focused on clarity, navigation, and scalable architecture.

Challenge

An outdated system that couldn't keep up

The existing Admin Dashboard at ProService was outdated, unintuitive, and failed to meet the evolving needs of HR administrators. Users expressed frustration with poor navigation, limited functionality, and a lack of actionable insights , hindering their ability to manage critical HR tasks efficiently.

These shortcomings not only impacted user satisfaction but also limited ProService's ability to differentiate in the market and achieve strategic goals for growth and adoption. A comprehensive redesign was imperative to address these challenges and position the platform as a cornerstone of ProService's growth strategy.

UX Research

Multi-method research grounded in real user behavior

I began with a heuristic evaluation of the existing dashboard to identify usability issues and inefficiencies. This was followed by stakeholder interviews with Subject Matter Experts, Sales, and Marketing teams to ensure alignment with strategic business goals and KPIs.

User interviews with 10 HR admins across various roles provided critical insights into workflows, frustrations, and desired features. Data analysis of support tickets, NPS summaries, and Salesforce data surfaced recurring challenges at scale, while competitive analysis identified best practices and differentiation opportunities.

Key Findings

Five patterns that shaped every design decision

01
Navigation inefficiency
Common tasks required excessive clicks. Admins spent more time finding tools than using them , a fundamental UX failure that affected daily productivity.
02
Buried critical data
Key metrics and reports were hidden under multiple navigation layers, making it difficult to retrieve actionable insights quickly when they were needed most.
03
No customization
The fixed interface couldn't adapt to different admin roles. Power users and occasional users had identical experiences, and neither was well-served.
04
Poor error handling
Frequent data input errors with no clear feedback or resolution paths. Admins were left without guidance when the system failed them.
05
Varied usage patterns
Some admins used the dashboard daily; others monthly for specific tasks. Both groups needed to be served without compromising either experience.
Wireframes

From research insights to structural solutions

Initial wireframes prioritized solving for the top user pain points: navigation inefficiency, data accessibility, and customization. Each structural decision was traced back to a specific research finding, ensuring the design was grounded in evidence rather than assumption.

Early wireframe exploring navigation hierarchy and quick-access panel structure
Solution

A scalable foundation, not a one-time fix

I led end-to-end design for the redesign, managing a designer to execute the visual system while I owned the UX strategy, information architecture, and design rationale. The focus was a modern, self-service experience that could scale without increasing headcount.

Streamlined navigation reduced clicks to reach critical tools. Favorites functionality allowed personalization for diverse admin roles. Employee Management tools were designed around the actual workflows uncovered in research. And an entirely new data visualization layer , charts, graphs, KPI widgets , surfaced actionable insights that previously required multiple steps to access.

Redesigned admin dashboard , streamlined navigation, surfaced KPIs, improved hierarchy
Figma Architecture

Built to extend, not to rebuild

The design system was architected in Figma with scalability in mind. Component patterns, spacing tokens, and interaction states were documented to ensure consistent execution across the redesign and to serve as a foundation for future feature work without requiring design from scratch.

Figma component library , scalable architecture for current and future feature sets
ProService Brand

Design that speaks the brand's language

The redesign was carefully aligned with ProService's visual identity , primary, secondary, and accent color palettes, typography, and UI conventions , ensuring credibility and consistency with the broader product ecosystem while introducing a modernized visual language.

Final Designs

The full admin portal, redesigned

The final design successfully bridges functionality and aesthetics, delivering a user-centered interface that directly addresses the pain points uncovered in research while aligning with ProService's strategic goals.

Outcome

Measurable improvement, positioned for growth

The redesign delivered a modern, streamlined interface that prioritized usability and data-driven decision-making. Simplified workflows reduced task completion time for key HR tasks. Improved data accessibility enabled faster, data-driven decisions. Tailored customization options improved adoption across both power users and occasional users.

Early feedback from internal stakeholders and admins indicated a significant improvement in user experience, with NPS improvements and increased platform engagement. The Figma architecture ensured the design team could continue iterating without rebuilding from scratch.
