The Admin Dashboard Redesign

ProService's Admin Dashboard redesign was a pivotal project aimed at modernizing the HR experience for administrators while aligning with strategic business objectives. The redesign introduced a streamlined interface and enhanced data-driven insights, creating a foundation for future applications. The result was a user-centered solution that balanced innovation with operational efficiency.

Challenge

An outdated dashboard failing its users

The existing Admin Dashboard at ProService was outdated, unintuitive, and failed to meet the evolving needs of HR administrators. Users expressed frustration with poor navigation, limited functionality, and a lack of actionable insights, hindering their ability to manage critical HR tasks efficiently.

These shortcomings not only impacted user satisfaction but also limited ProService's ability to differentiate itself in the market and achieve its strategic goals for growth and adoption.

Solution

A modernized experience aligned to business goals

The primary goal of the Admin Dashboard redesign was to modernize the user experience for HR administrators while aligning with ProService's strategic objectives. This included driving growth by increasing market adoption, enhancing customer value through improved functionality, and improving overall customer satisfaction.

The redesign introduced an intuitive interface, actionable insights, and scalable architecture, establishing a strong foundation for future product iterations.

Approach

User-centered from day one

To tackle the challenges of the Admin Dashboard redesign, a user-centered design approach was employed. This began with in-depth research, including interviews with ProService employees, subject matter experts, and HR administrators to uncover pain points and desired functionalities.

Additional insights were gathered from NPS scores, Salesforce data, and support emails to form a comprehensive understanding of user needs. Collaboration was key throughout the process, involving cross-functional teams such as product management, development, and branding to align the redesign with technical capabilities and business objectives. Initial wireframes were created to address user priorities, with iterative improvements made to balance innovation with operational feasibility.

UX Research

Multi-method research to understand the full picture

To create a user-centered redesign of the ProService Admin Dashboard, I conducted comprehensive UX research to understand the needs, challenges, and behaviors of HR administrators. This research laid the foundation for informed design decisions that aligned with both user expectations and business objectives.

I began with a heuristic evaluation of the existing dashboard to identify usability issues and inefficiencies. Stakeholder interviews with Subject Matter Experts, Sales, and Marketing teams ensured alignment with strategic business goals and KPIs. User interviews with 10 HR admins provided critical insights into workflows, frustrations, and desired features. Data analysis of support tickets, NPS summaries, and Salesforce data surfaced recurring challenges at scale, while competitive analysis identified best practices and differentiation opportunities.

User Interviews

Five patterns that shaped every decision

01
Navigation issues
Admins struggled with finding key tools and information due to inefficient navigation. Excessive clicks and poor organization slowed down their workflows.
02
Data accessibility
Critical data, such as employee metrics and reports, was often buried under multiple layers, making it hard to retrieve actionable insights quickly.
03
Customizability lacking
The existing dashboard lacked flexibility to adapt to specific user workflows, leading to frustration among admins with diverse needs.
04
Frequent errors
Admins encountered recurring errors with data input and retrieval, often with no clear feedback or resolution, further hindering productivity.
05
Task-specific usage
Some admins used the dashboard daily while others used it occasionally for specific tasks. This variability required a design that could serve both without compromising either.
Wireframes

Translating research into structure

The wireframe proposed a complete restructure of the ProService Admin Dashboard, focused on enhancing usability, improving data accessibility, and aligning with user needs and business objectives.

Header Navigation provides quick access to essential elements including a Company Selection Dropdown for multi-company management, direct access to Time and Attendance, and a user profile with settings. Quick Access Panels surface frequently accessed pages such as Time Sheet Entry, Employee Details, and Payroll Summary, while Recommended Pages provide contextually relevant suggestions based on user activity. Data Visualizations include interactive widgets for Billed Invoices, Employee Count Breakdown, Turnover Percentage, Supervisor-to-Worker Ratio, PTO Bank Liability, and Annualized Salary, each designed to surface actionable insights at a glance.

Early wireframe exploring navigation hierarchy and quick-access panel structure
Figma Architecture

Built to scale, not just to ship

The original Admin Dashboard lacked the usability and functionality required to support administrators effectively, resulting in inefficiencies and user frustration. Key pain points included a cluttered interface, inconsistent navigation, and limited capabilities for critical tasks like payroll and employee management.

The Figma architecture was built with scalability in mind. Component patterns, spacing tokens, and interaction states were documented to ensure consistent execution across the redesign and to serve as a foundation for future feature work without requiring work from scratch.

ProService Brand

Design that speaks the brand's language

To ensure consistency and alignment with ProService's visual identity, the redesigned Admin Dashboard was carefully crafted to reflect the brand's established guidelines, including its primary, secondary, and accent color palettes. Typography, iconography, and UI conventions were maintained to enhance credibility and reinforce trust across the platform.

Designs

The final admin portal

The final design of the ProService Admin Dashboard successfully bridges functionality and aesthetics, delivering a user-centered interface that directly aligns with ProService's strategic goals of improving efficiency, increasing customer satisfaction, and driving market adoption.

Streamlined User Experience: improved navigation and visual hierarchy reduce task completion times, directly addressing user pain points identified during research. Enhanced Data Accessibility: key metrics and insights are surfaced through intuitive visualizations, enabling faster data-driven decision-making. Customization for Diverse Users: the ability to tailor dashboards for individual roles ensures the interface meets the needs of both power users and occasional users.

Outcome

Measurable impact on users and the business

The redesigned Admin Dashboard introduced a modern, streamlined interface that prioritized usability and data-driven decision-making. Key features included enhanced navigation, improved Favorites functionality, and intuitive Employee Management tools, all tailored to address user feedback. The redesign incorporated visual data insights such as charts and graphs to align with ProService's strategic objective of offering new functionality.

The redesign aligned with ProService's strategic objectives by driving customer satisfaction and increasing engagement with the platform. Navigation improvements reduced task completion time for HR admins, allowing them to focus on high-value activities. Early feedback from internal stakeholders and admins indicated a significant improvement in user experience, positioning ProService as a leader in HR solutions.
