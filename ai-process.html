Researching the Modern
AI Design Process

Before starting a major portfolio project, I spent weeks researching and validating how elite design teams actually work in 2025–2026. What started as a 14-phase sequential map ended as a fundamental rethinking of how AI-native design process should be structured , and why most designers are getting it wrong.

Context

The right question before the first pixel

Most designers add AI tools to their existing workflow and call it "AI-native." That's not what elite teams at Stripe, Linear, Ramp, and Vercel are doing. Before starting a major portfolio project demonstrating modern AI design process, I needed to know exactly what that process actually looks like in practice , not what articles say it looks like.

I spent weeks conducting deep-dive research: reading interviews with designers at these companies, analyzing published process documentation, and synthesizing findings through multiple validation rounds. This is a case study about that research itself , the thinking before the building.

The Staff/Principal signal is doing this work before touching a tool. Validating the methodology first, not figuring it out as you go.

Finding 01

The real distinction: AI as veneer vs. AI as infrastructure

The first major finding from the research was structural. The question isn't which AI tools you use , it's whether AI is integrated into the pipeline or layered on top of it. These are fundamentally different approaches with different outcomes.

The Core Insight

Elite teams aren't just using AI tools , they've rebuilt how design and engineering connect. The bottleneck was never generating pixels. It was always the gap between design intent and shipped code. AI-as-infrastructure closes that gap.

Finding 02

14 phases → 6 zones: the structural shift

The initial process map had 14 sequential phases , correct in content but wrong in structure. Research into how Stripe, Linear, Ramp, and Vercel actually work confirmed that AI compresses these activities into concurrent zones, not discrete steps. The phases don't disappear; they collapse into each other.

Finding 03

The validated framework: 6 zones, end-to-end

The final framework maps each zone to its core activities and tools. This is the operational blueprint , what actually happens within each zone, not just what it's called. The design-to-code pipeline running through Zones 4 and 5 is the critical infrastructure that separates AI-as-veneer from AI-as-infrastructure.
